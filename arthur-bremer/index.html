Average game length, per team 1990-2015

By Arthur Cook Bremer
Published May 5, 2016

At the start of the 2015 season, MLB implented new rules to shorten the length of games. Since the pace of game rules were applied, we've already seen a drop in average game time from a record high during the 2014 season.

The team with the longest average game time for a season within this 25-year period was the 2014 Boston Red Sox at 197 minutes. The team with the shortest average game time for a season was the 2003 Detroit Tigers at 159 minutes.

There was a noticeable drop in the average length of games in the mid-2000s before beginning to rise again. The earlier peak in average game length may be attributed to more offensive runs being scored per game, but as game length peaked to a new high in 2014, teams were averaging fewer runs, which may have inspired the new pace rules, according to an article by CBS sports writer Dayn Perry.

Source: Baseball Reference
