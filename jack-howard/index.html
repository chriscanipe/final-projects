"Grade Inflation" May Be Less Prevalent At MU Than Many SEC schools

By Jack Howard

MU GPA has increased 0.20 points since 1997, according to the University's online records. And that rise fits with a national trend of nearly 0.1 points per decade since 1983, said former Duke professor Stuart Rojstaczer. Many other SEC schools, like Alabama (0.49), have increased more in the same period of time, according to public GPA data. According to Rojstaczer's research, grade inflation is a rise in GPA as a result of increasingly lenient grading structures. He said it began in the 1980s when students began to grade their professors with course evaluations at the end of the year that affect professor's tenure and classes next semester. Rojstaczer is a geophysicist and writer who's published two papers on grade inflation.

Published May 3, 2016
Sources: MU Registrar Office, GradeInflation.com and Stuart Rojstaczer.
